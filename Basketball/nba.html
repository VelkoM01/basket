NBA

The National Basketball Association, better known by the abbreviation NBA, is the strongest professional basketball league in the world and one of the main professional sports leagues in North America. Currently, 29 clubs from the USA and 1 from Canada participate in it. The league was founded in New York on June 6, 1946 under the name Basketball Association of America, abbreviated BAA. It adopted the name National Basketball Association in the fall of 1949 after merging with the rival National Basketball League. The National Basketball League trademark or NBA logo was created by Alan Siegel, and the NBA League trademark is pictured by basketball player Jerry West. The team with the most trophies is the Boston Celtics, who have 17 titles, and right behind them are the Los Angeles Lakers with 16 titles.
